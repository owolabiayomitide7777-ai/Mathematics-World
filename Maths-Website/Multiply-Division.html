Lesson Note — Multiplication and Division

Lesson Objectives

  • Define multiplication and division of numbers.

  • Apply correct methods for multiplying and dividing whole numbers.

  • Solve real-life problems involving multiplication and division.

  • Understand and use the relationship between multiplication and division.

Lesson Content

  • Multiplication: Multiplication is one of the four basic arithmetic operations.

  • It is simply repeated addition.

    Example: 4 × 3 = 4 + 4 + 4 = 12

  • The result of multiplication is called the product.

  • The numbers multiplied together are called factors or multiplicands.

  • Multiplier: the number of groups.

  • Division: Division means sharing a number (the dividend) into equal groups determined by another number (the divisor).

  • The answer to a division problem is called the quotient, and sometimes there is a remainder.

  • Multiplication and division are inverse operations.

  • Dividend ÷ Divisor = Quotient (and Remainder)

Worked Examples

Example 1 (Multiplication): Multiply 234 by 12

Step 1: 234 × 2 = 468

Step 2: 234 × 10 = 2340

Step 3: Add 468 + 2340 = 2808

Answer: 2808

Example 2 (Division): Divide 567 by 7

Step 1: 7 into 56 → 8

Step 2: Bring down 7 → 7 ÷ 7 = 1

Answer: 81

Example 3: How many tins of milk are there in 978 cartons, if there are 36 tins in a carton?

Step 1: 978 x 6 = 5868

Step 2: 978 x 30 = 29340

Step 3: 5868 + 29340 = 35208

Answer: 35208

Example 4: 50,000 mangoes were shared equally among 130 men and 120 women. How many mangoes did each person receive?

Step 1: 3. The total number of men and women is:

130 + 120

= 250

Step 2: 50,000 ÷ 250

Step 3: 250 into 50,000 → 200

Answer: 200

Assessment on Multiplication and Division

  3. What is 4,123 times 21?

  4. Find the product of 256 and 124

  5. Multiply 218 by 15

Section B: Simple Division

  1. 1440 ÷ 12

  2. 156 ÷ 13

  3. 567 ÷ 7

  4. 324 ÷ 18

  5. 403 ÷ 23

  6. 2108 ÷ 124

Section C: Word Problems

  1. A farmer has 224 boxes of oranges. Each box contains 36 oranges. How many oranges does he have in total?

  2. 4800 books are shared equally among 8 students. How many books does each student get?

  3. A car travels 65 km per hour. How far will it travel in 36 hours?

  4. A factory produces 1,200 bottles in 8 hours. How many bottles per hour?

  5. A rope of 240 m is cut into pieces of 15 m each. How many pieces are obtained?
