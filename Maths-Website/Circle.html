Lesson Note — Perimeter and Area of a Circle

Learning Objectives

  • Define a circle and and its components (radius, diameter, chord, sector, perimeter, sector, semi-circle).

  • Identify radius (r) and diameter (d).

  • Use formula: C = 2πr or C = πd

  • Calculate the perimeter of a circle.

  • Solve word problems involving perimeter.

  • Recall the formula for the area of a circle.

  • Calculate the area of a circle given the radius or diameter.

  • Solve real-life problems involving the area of a circle.

Lesson Content

  • A circle is a perfect round plane figure or a set of points equidistant from the center. It is a common shape.

    Examples of a circular shapes are coins, wheels, rings, tyres, tins, top and bottom of buckets.

  • Centre(c):This is a point at the middle of the circle.

  • Circumference or Perimeter(P): This is the curved outer edge of a circle(or the distance across the circle).

    It is the longest part of a circle.

    C = 2πr or C = πd, where π ≈ 3.142 or 22/7.

  • Diameter(D): This a line from a side throught the centre to another side. It is a straight that divides the distance around the circle.

    D = 2 x r

  • Radius(R):This is any straight line drawn from the centre of a circle to the circumference.

    r = (diameter) / 2

  • Area of a circle(A): This is the space inside the circle.

    A = π r², π ≈ 3.142 or 22/7

  • Semi-circle is half of a circle.

  • A quarter part of a circle is called a Quadrant

  • Chord: This is a line joining two points on the circumference. Unlike the diameter, a chord does not divide the circle into two equal parts.

  • Sector:This is a region between two radii and the circumference.

Worked Examples

Example 1: If the radius of a circle is given as 7 cm, find perimeter.

C = 2 × 3.142 × 7

= 43.988

≈ 44 cm

Answer: 44cm

Example 2: If the diameter of a circle is 20 cm, find the circumference.

C = 3.142 × 20

= 62.84

≈ 63 cm

Answer: 63cm

Example 3: find the circumference of a circle with radius = 5 m. Take π = 22/7, find perimeter.

C = 2 × 22/7 × 5

= 220/7

≈ 31.43 m

Answer: 31.43m

Example 4: Find the area of a circle of radius = 7 cm

A = 3.142 × 7²

= 3.142 × 49

= 153.958

≈ 154 cm²

Answer: 154 cm²

Example 5: Calculate the area of a circle of diameter = 10 cm

r = 10/2 = 5 cm

A = 3.142 × 5²

= 3.142 × 25

= 78.55

≈ 78.6 cm²

Answer:78.6 cm²

Example 6: If the radius of a circle is 14 m, calculate the area of a circle. (π = 22/7)

A = 22/7 × 14²

= 22/7 × 196

= 22 × 28

= 616 m²

Answer: 616 m²
