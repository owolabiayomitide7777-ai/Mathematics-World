Lesson Note — Prime Numbers and Prime Factors

Lesson Objectives

  • Define prime and composite numbers.

  • Identify prime numbers up to 100.

  • Use prime numbers in factorization and problem-solving.

  • Define prime factor.

  • Express a number as a product of prime factors.

  • Use prime factorization to solve HCF and LCM problems.

  • Solve word problems involving prime factors.

Lesson Content

  • Prime Number: A prime number is a number that can only be divided by 1 and itself only (1 and itself).

    Note: 1 is not a prime number since it does not have two different factors.

  • Composite Number: A composite number is a number that has more than two factors.

  • 1 is neither prime nor composite.

  • Prime numbers are used in HCF, LCM, and cryptography.

  • Prime Factor: A prime number that divides exactly into a given number.

  • Every number can be expressed as a product of prime numbers.

  • Prime factorization helps in HCF, LCM, and simplifying fractions.

Examples

Prime numbers less than 20: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19

Composite numbers less than 20: 4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16, 18


Methods to Find Prime Factors

Method 1: Factor Tree

Example: 24 →
24
/ \
6 4
/ \ / \
2 3 2 2
Prime factors: 2 × 2 × 2 × 3

Answer: 2 x 2 x 2 x 3

Method 2: Division Method

Example: 36 → 36 ÷ 2 = 18; 18 ÷ 2 = 9; 9 ÷ 3 = 3; 3 ÷ 3 = 1 → Prime factors: 2 × 2 × 3 × 3

Answer: 2 x 2 x 3 x 3
