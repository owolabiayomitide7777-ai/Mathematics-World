Lesson Note — HCF

Lesson Objectives

  • Define HCF.

  • Find HCF of two or more numbers using listing factors, prime factorization, or division method.

  • Solve real-life problems involving HCF.

lesson Content

  • HCF: Highest Common factor(HCF) is the largest number that divides exactly into two or more numbers.

  • Useful in sharing things equally, reducing fractions, and simplifying ratios.

Terms for Highest Common Factor are:

  1. The Highest Number

  2. The Grearest Number

  3. The Biggest Number

  4. The Longest Length

  5. The Largest Number

Methods to Find HCF

Method 1: Listing Factors

Example: Find the highest common factor of 12 and 18.

Factors of 12 are: 1,2,3,4,6,12;

Factors of 18 are: 1,2,3,6,9,18;

Common: 1,2,3,6

HCF = 6

Answer: 6

Method 2: Prime Factorization

Example: Find the highest common factor of 24 and 36.

24 = 2×2×2×3;

36 = 2×2×3×3;

Common factors = 2×2×3 = 12

→ HCF = 12

Answer: 12

Method 3: Division Method

Example: Find the highest common factor of 48 and 18.

→ 48 ÷ 18 = 2 rem 12;

18 ÷ 12 = 1 rem 6;

12 ÷ 6 = 2 rem 0

→ HCF = 6

Answer: 6

Method 4: Prime Factors Method

Example: Express the highest common factor of the following in prime factor:

2 x 3 x 3

3 x 3 x 3

3 x 3 x 5

The common factors are: 3 x 3

The highest common factor = 3 x 3

Answer: 3 x 3

  1. Two ropes of lengths 48 m and 60 m are cut into equal pieces. Find the longest possible length of each piece.

  2. Two containers have 72 and 108 liters of water. How many liters should be in each container if they must be equal?

  3. A teacher has 36 red and 54 blue pencils. She wants to pack them equally. How many pencils in each pack?

  4. Two trains arrive at a station every 12 minutes and 18 minutes. After how many minutes will they arrive together?
