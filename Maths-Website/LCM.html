Lesson Note — LCM

Lesson Objectives

  • Define LCM.

  • Find LCM of two or more numbers using listing multiples, prime factorization, or division method.

  • Solve real-life problems involving LCM.

Lesson Content

  • LCM: Lowest Common Multiple is the smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

  • Useful in synchronizing events, adding fractions, and planning schedules.

Terms for Lowest Common Multiple are:

  1. The Least Number

  2. The Smallest Number

  3. The LowestNumber

Methods to Find LCM

Method 1: Listing Multiples

Example: Find the lowest common multiple 4 and 6.

→ Multiples of 4: 4,8,12,…;

Multiples of 6: 6,12,18,…;

Common: 12,24,…

→ LCM = 12

Answer: 12

Method 2: Prime Factorization

Example:Find the lowest common multiple of 12 and 18

12 = 2²×3¹,

18 = 2¹×3²;

LCM = 2²×3² = 36

Answer: 36

Method 3: Division (Ladder) Method

Example: Find the lowest common multiple of 8 and 12.

2 | 8 12

2 | 4 6

2 | 2 3

3 | 1 3

| 1 1

LCM = 2×2×2×3

= 24

Answer: 24

Section D: Word Problems

  1. Two buses leave a station at intervals of 12 and 18 minutes. After how many minutes will they leave together?

  2. A gardener waters plants every 4 days and fertilizes every 6 days. After how many days will both activities occur on the same day?

  3. Two machines produce items every 8 and 12 hours. When will they produce items together?

  4. A teacher distributes books every 5 days and pens every 3 days. After how many days will both distributions happen together?

  5. Two lights blink at intervals of 6 and 9 seconds. When will they blink together?
