Lesson Note — Multiplication and Division of Fraction

Lesson Objectives

  • Define Fraction.

  • Multiply two or more fractions together.

  • Divide one fraction by another fraction.

Lesson Content

Addition and Subtraction of Fractions

A fraction is a part of a whole. It is written in the form a / b where:

  • a is the numerator (top number)

  • b is the denominator (bottom number)

A. Multiplication of Fractions

To multiply fractions:

  1. Multiply the numerators.

  2. Multiply the denominators.

  3. Simplify the answer if possible.

Examples

Example 1: Simplify (2 / 5) x (3 / 4)

Solution

  • Numerators: 2 x 3 = 6

  • Denominator: 5 x 4 = 20

  • So, (2/5) x (3/4) = 6/20 = 3/10(simplified)

    • Answer: 3/10

Example 2: Evaluate (3 / 7) x (14 / 9)

Solution

Cross-cancel first:

  • 14 ÷ 7 = 2

  • 9 ÷ 3 = 3

  • So, (1/1) x (2/3)

    = 2/3

Answer: 2/3

B. Divide of Fractions

To divide fractions:

  1. Change division sign (÷) to multiplication (x).

  2. Convert (remane) the fractions to have the same denominator.

  3. Flip the second fraction (find its reciprocal).

  4. Multiply the fractions.

  5. Simplify the answer if needed.

Example 3: Evaluate (4 / 9) ÷ (2 / 3)

Solution

Step 1: Change to Multiplication and flip the second fraction: 4/9 x 3/2

Step 2: Multiply:

  • Numerators: 4 x 3 = 12

  • Denominators: 9 x 2 = 18

  • 12 / 18

    = 2/3

Answer: 2/3

Example 4: Evaluate (5 / 6) ÷ (1 / 4)

Solution

Step 1: Change to Multiplication and flip the second fraction: 5/6 x 4/1

Step 2: Multiply:

  • Numerators: 5 x 4 = 20

  • Denominators: 6 x 1 = 6

  • 20 / 6

    = 10/3

    = 3 (1/3)

Answer: 3 (1/3)
