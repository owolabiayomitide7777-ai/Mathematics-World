Lesson Note — Addition and Subtraction of Metric Lengths

Lesson Objectives

  • Define Length

  • Convert from one unit to another unit

  • Add two or more metric lenghs together.

  • Subtract one metric length from another metric length.

Lesson Content

    The metric units of length include:

    `
  • Millimeter (mm)

  • Centimeter (cm)

  • Meter (m)

  • Kilometer (km)

Conversions:

  • 10 mm = 1 cm

  • 100cm = 1 m

  • 1000cm = 1 km

How to Add Metric Length

To add metric length:

  1. Write all values in the same unit.

  2. Add the numbers

  3. Convert back to required unit if necessary.

Examples

Example 1: Add 2m 35cm and 1m 45cm

Solution

  • Convert to same unit (cm)

  • 2m 35cm = (2 x 100) + 35 = 235 cm

  • 1m 45cm = (1 x100) + 45 = 145 cm

  • 235cm + 145cm = 380cm

  • Convert back: 380cm = 3m 80cm

Answer: 3m 80cm

How to Subtract Metric Length

To subtract metric length:

  1. Convert to the same unit.

  2. Subtract normally.

  3. Convert back if needed.

Example 2:Subtract 3m 20cm from 5m.

Solution:

  • convert to cm:

  • 5m = (5 x 100) = 500cm

  • 3m 20cm = (3 x 100) + 20 = 320cm

  • 500cm - 320cm = 180cm

  • Convert back: 180m = 1m 80cm

Answer: 1m 80cm

  4. A boy travelled 1km 500m on foot, 4km 250m on bicycle and the rest 12km 560m by car. How far did he go?

  5. The two parallel sides of a rectangular fence measures 10m each and he other parallel sides measure 2m 96m each. What is the sum of all the sides?

  6. What is the sum of 4km 500m, 3km 700m and 980m?

Section B: Subtraction of Metric length

  1. Find the difference between 9km 290m and 2km 709m

  2. Find the difference 5km 310m and 3km 506m

  3. Deduct 23m 44cm 6mm from 45m 38cm 3mm

  4. Subtract 4km 201m from 6km

  5. From a piece of cloth measuring 5 metres, a tailor cuts 1 metre 25 centimetres of it. What is left?

  6. What is left of 4 kilometres after a cyclist had covered 450 metres?
