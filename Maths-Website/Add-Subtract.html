Lesson Note — Addition and Subtraction of Numbers

Learning Objectives

  • Define addition and subtraction of numbers.

  • Apply correct methods for adding and subtracting whole numbers and decimals.

  • Solve real-life problems involving addition and subtraction.

  • Use mental and written strategies effectively.

Lesson Content

  • Addition: Addition is the combining numbers to get a sum or total.

  • Subtraction: Subtraction is finding the difference or removing part of a number.

  • Always align place values for accuracy.

Terms for Addition are:

  1. Sum

  2. Total

  3. Altogether

  4. Increase

  5. Population

  6. More Than

Terms for Subtraction are:

  1. Deduct

  2. Takeaway

  3. Difference between

  4. Fewer than

  5. Decrease

  6. Minus

  7. Reduce

Worked Examples

Example 1: 456 + 378 = ?

Step 1: Add units: 6 + 8 = 14 → write 4, carry 1

Step 2: Add tens: 5 + 7 + 1 = 13 → write 3, carry 1

Step 3: Add hundreds: 4 + 3 + 1 = 8

Answer: 834

Example 2: 762 - 485 = ?

Step 1: Subtract units: 2 - 5 → borrow 1 → 12 - 5 = 7

Step 2: Subtract tens: 5 - 8 → borrow 1 → 15 - 8 = 7

Step 3: Subtract hundreds: 6 - 4 = 2

Answer: 277

Example 3: What number must Mr. Owolabi add to 97,500 to become 1,000,000?

1,000,000 - 97500

= 9 0 2 5 0 0

Answer: 902500

Example 4: There are 22,000 people in a town. 6,800 are men, 9,900 are women and the rest are children. How many children are there?

Step 1: The sum of Men and Women is:

6,800 + 9,900

= 1 6,7 0 0

Step 2: The number of children is:

22,000 - 16,700

= 5,3 0 0

Answer: 5,300
