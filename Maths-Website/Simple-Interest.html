Lesson Note — Simple Interest and Maturity Value

Lesson Objectives

  • Define simple interest.

  • Identify and explain Principal (P), Rate (R), Time (T).

  • Use the formula SI = (P × R × T)/100.

  • Calculate simple interest correctly.

  • Solve real-life problems involving simple interest.

  • Define maturity value.

  • Identify components of maturity value (Principal and Simple Interest).

  • Use the formula Maturity Value(MV) = Principal(P) + Simple Interest(SI) and calculate maturity value from P, R and T.

  • Solve real-life problems involving maturity value.

Lesson Content

  • Principal (P): Amount borrowed or invested.

  • Rate (R): Interest percentage per year.

  • Time (T): Number of years money is borrowed/invested.

  • Simple Interest Formula: SI = (P × R × T)/100

  • Meaning: Maturity Value (MV) is the total amount received at the end of an investment or loan period — it equals the principal plus interest.

  • Formula:SI = (P × R × T) / 100

    MV = P + SI

  • Procedure: find SI using the formula above, then add to P to get MV.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Calculate the simple interest on ₦5,000 at 10% for 2 years.

SI = (5000 × 10 × 2)/100

= ₦1,000

Answer: ₦1,000

Example 2: Find the simple interest on ₦12,000 at 8% for 3 years.

SI = (12000 × 8 × 3)/100

= ₦2,880

Answer: ₦2,880

Example 3: Find the simple interest on ₦15,000 at 5% for 4 years.

SI = (15000 × 5 × 4)/100

= ₦3,000

Answer: ₦3,000

Example 4: Find the maturity value of ₦5,000 invested for 2 years at 10%.

SI = (5000 × 10 × 2) / 100

= ₦1,000

MV = 5000 + 1000

= ₦6,000

Answer: ₦6,000

Example 5: Find the maturity value of ₦12,000 borrowed at 8% for 3 years.

SI = (12000 × 8 × 3) / 100

= ₦2,880

MV = 12000 + 2,880

= ₦14,880

Answer: ₦14,880

Example 6: Find the maturity value of ₦15,000 after 4 years at 5%.

SI = (15000 × 5 × 4) / 100

= ₦3,000

MV = 15000 + 3,000

= ₦18,000

Answer: ₦18,000
